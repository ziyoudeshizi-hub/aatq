## Key Finding
All ternary configurations converge to 35.1-36.2% average accuracy,
which matches the random-chance baseline when accounting for model biases.
This is INDEPENDENT of:
- Model architecture (TinyLlama 1.1B vs Qwen 0.5B)
- Learning rate (0.02, 0.005, 0.0005)
- Training steps (300, 500, 1000)
- Selective quantization (skip first/last 2 layers)
- Knowledge distillation (T=2, alpha=0.9)
- Group size (128 vs 32, i.e., 4x more parameters)

## Training Hyperparameters
- Calibration: 128 sequences x 128 tokens from C4
- Optimizer: Adam (beta1=0.9, beta2=0.99, eps=1e-4)
- Gradient clipping: max_norm=1.0
- Tau schedule: cosine from 1.0 to 0.25
- Warmup: 20 steps linear
- Best-state checkpointing: always restores best loss state

## Conclusion
Post-training ternary quantization fundamentally fails on sub-1.5B models.
The information bottleneck (1.58 bpw) is too aggressive to preserve
discriminative reasoning capabilities at this model scale.
